The science of intensive therapy

Why a marriage intensive works when weekly therapy hasn't

The therapeutic research is clear: sustained, concentrated intervention creates neurological and relational shifts that fragmented weekly sessions can't match.

01

No More Resetting

In weekly therapy, you spend the first 15 minutes catching up and the last 10 winding down. That leaves 25 minutes of actual work — and seven days for old patterns to solidify before the next session. An intensive eliminates the reset entirely.

02

Breakthrough Depth

Real therapeutic breakthroughs require sustained emotional engagement. Hour three of an intensive reaches depths that hour one of a weekly session never will. You get past the surface, past the defenses, into the material that actually changes things.

03

Momentum, Not Maintenance

Weekly therapy often becomes maintenance — managing symptoms without transforming the underlying dynamic. An intensive creates escape velocity. You don't just manage the crisis. You fundamentally restructure how you relate to each other.

Side by side

Weekly Therapy vs. Marriage Intensive

Both have value. But for couples in crisis, the math is different.

Weekly Sessions
Traditional format. Effective for lower-acuity issues over time.
  • 50 minutes of session time per week
  • 15+ minutes lost to warm-up and wind-down
  • 7 days between sessions for patterns to re-entrench
  • 6–12 months for meaningful progress on complex issues
  • Difficult to sustain emotional depth at surface-level pacing
Marriage Intensive
Concentrated intervention for couples who can't wait months.
  • 4–12+ hours of sustained therapeutic work
  • Full engagement from the first moment — no warm-up tax
  • Continuous momentum builds breakthroughs in real time
  • Achieve in one day what takes 3–6 months in weekly format
  • Private, one-on-one — no group retreat, no strangers
Your intensive, step by step

What a day with me looks like

Every intensive is custom-built around your specific crisis. But here's the structure that guides the day.

1

Pre-Intensive Assessment

Before your session

Both partners complete in-depth assessments before we meet. I review your history, your patterns, and your goals so we start with clarity — not a cold open. We hit the ground running.

2

Mapping the Core Dynamic

Morning session

We identify the negative cycle that's running your marriage — the pattern underneath every fight, every shutdown, every wall. This isn't surface-level. We go to the root.

3

Structured Intervention

Core therapeutic work

Using evidence-based interventions tailored to your specific crisis, we restructure communication, process unresolved injuries, and rebuild the emotional connection that's been eroded.

4

Skill Building & Integration

Afternoon

Breakthroughs mean nothing without infrastructure. We build practical tools and frameworks you'll use immediately — specific to your patterns, not generic worksheets.

5

Your Roadmap Forward

Closing & follow-up plan

You leave with a clear, concrete plan — what to do this week, what to watch for, and when we check in next. The intensive isn't the end. It's the turning point.
