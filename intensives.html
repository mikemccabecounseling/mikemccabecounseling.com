Understanding the format

What is a marriage intensive?

A marriage intensive is a concentrated block of couples therapy — typically a half-day, full day, or two consecutive days — designed to accomplish in hours what traditional weekly therapy takes months to achieve. Unlike a weekly 50-minute session, an intensive gives you uninterrupted time to go deep into the patterns, ruptures, and dynamics driving your relationship crisis.

At Mike McCabe Counseling, every intensive is private — just you, your partner, and me. There are no group workshops, no pre-packaged curriculum. The work is tailored entirely to your relationship using Relational Life Therapy (RLT) and Gottman-informed methods. Both partners complete individual assessments beforehand so we hit the ground running, and every intensive includes follow-up sessions to reinforce the work.

Marriage intensives are especially effective for couples dealing with infidelity, trust ruptures, emotional disconnection, or the prospect of separation — situations where the urgency demands more than one hour a week.
